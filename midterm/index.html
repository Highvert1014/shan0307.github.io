History of the NBA

The National Basketball Association (NBA) was founded in 1946 as the Basketball Association of America (BAA). It later merged with the National Basketball League (NBL) to form the NBA in 1949. Over the decades, the league has grown into a global powerhouse, featuring legendary players like Michael Jordan, LeBron James, Kobe Bryant, and Stephen Curry. With intense rivalries, high-flying dunks, and unforgettable championship moments, the NBA remains a beloved sport worldwide.

Interesting NBA Facts

  • The NBA was officially founded in 1946 as the BAA before merging with the NBL in 1949.
  • Wilt Chamberlain holds the record for most points in a single game with 100.
  • Michael Jordan won six NBA championships with the Chicago Bulls.
  • The Boston Celtics hold the most NBA championships with 17 titles.
  • LeBron James is the youngest player to score 30,000 career points.
